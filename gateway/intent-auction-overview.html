AgentCart Intent And Quote Market

The useful analogy is intents, not ads: a household states constrained demand, merchants answer with signed quotes, and the user's agent ranks locally by policy before any payment happens.

Safe Flow

1. Intent

Household Need

Buy tea, detergent, or coffee under exact rules: country, max price, category, delivery window, approval threshold.

2. Registry

Find Shops

Read public merchant records and /.well-known/agentcart.json. No demand is published onchain.

3. Catalog

Filter Products

Only shops that ship to the target country and expose matching categories enter the quote round.

4. Quote

Private Competition

Selected shops return final signed quotes: total price, VAT, shipping, stock, merchant of record, ETA.

5. Rank

User Policy Wins

The agent ranks locally by user policy. Cheapest is not always best; local/trusted/faster can win.

6. Pay

MPP / x402

Payment proof is bound to quote hash, merchant, amount, currency, selected rail, and rail-specific recipient/profile fields.

7. Order

Merchant Order

WooCommerce creates the order. Status/tracking stays with the merchant and is exposed back to the agent.

MEV / Intent Similarity

Similar Pattern

  • User expresses an outcome, not every execution step.
  • Solver/searcher/merchant side competes to satisfy exact constraints.
  • The final execution should be bound to the signed intent/quote.
  • Replay protection and transparent settlement proof matter.

Important Difference

  • This is consumer commerce, so privacy, returns, VAT, delivery, and consent matter.
  • Ranking must not be controlled by whoever pays most for attention.
  • Household demand should not be broadcast publicly.
  • Merchant of record stays responsible for fulfillment and support.

Registry Design

Onchain Anchor

Domain, merchant id, manifest URL, manifest hash, supported protocol versions, payment network, recipient address.

Offchain Manifest

Catalog endpoint, quote endpoint, order endpoint, shipping countries, public support contact, policy capabilities.

Private Quote Request

Destination country/postcode, quantity, timing, preferences, and household policy stay client-side or relay-private.

The registry proves where to ask. It must not decide who wins.

Market Rules

Compete On Quotes

Merchants compete on final price, shipping, stock, ETA, and trust. This is useful competition.

No Paid Ranking

Do not let shops buy top placement for generic searches. That turns the system into an ad network.

User-Owned Ranking

The household agent explains why a quote won: total cost, delivery, verified merchant, local preference, policy fit.

No Public Demand Leak

Do not broadcast “this household needs detergent in this area.” Ask only selected merchants or privacy-preserving relays.

Small Shop Counterweights

Expose local, independent, pickup, sustainability, and trust signals so small shops can win on more than price.

Verifier Is Required

x402 or MPP pays; it does not by itself solve catalog discovery, VAT quote, delivery terms, approval, or order tracking.
