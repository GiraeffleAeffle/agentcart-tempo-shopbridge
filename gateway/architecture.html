AgentCart System Architecture

AgentCart is a household safety and merchant adapter layer around MPP HTTP-auth checkout. It makes opt-in merchants agent-readable without making them implement every agent protocol on day one.

Household Control

Home Assistant triggers, explicit approval, policy checks, and auditability stay local.

Agent API

Agents get a stable surface for search, quote, approval, checkout, order state, and audit logs.

Merchant Adapter

Opt-in shops expose catalog, stock, quotes, orders, and fulfillment through a simple adapter.

Payment Boundary

MPP/x402 belongs at checkout. Catalog, policy, approval, and orders are commerce-layer concerns.

Purchase Flow

  1. 1
    Home Assistant detects low tea stock
    A sensor, button, or automation creates the reason for the purchase.
  2. 2
    Household agent searches and quotes
    The agent calls AgentCart catalog and quote endpoints, not third-party shop websites.
  3. 3
    Policy and approval gate the order
    Budget, category, merchant allowlist, delivery region, approval token, and expiry are checked before payment.
  4. 4
    Checkout uses HTTP Payment authentication
    402 Payment Required returns a payment challenge; the retry uses Authorization: Payment.
  5. 5
    Order, Vikunja, and audit trail are written
    The merchant order, task update, and audit events explain why the purchase happened.

Safety Boundaries

  • !
    No hidden browser purchasing
    Only opt-in merchants or demo shops should be connected.
  • !
    Merchant remains merchant of record
    Tax, invoice, fulfillment, returns, refunds, and support stay with the merchant.
  • !
    No custody in AgentCart
    AgentCart should not collect money and later pay the merchant unless you intentionally become a marketplace/payment intermediary.
  • !
    Replay protection
    Checkout binds quote, approval, amount, digest, idempotency key, and a single-use payment challenge.
Recommended demo

Test-mode payment adapter

Use official MPP testnet tooling or the current demo payment adapter. It proves the full architecture without live funds, chargebacks, tax exposure, or fulfillment liability.

Possible with setup

Live opt-in merchant payment

Replace the demo adapter with an official MPP method such as Tempo, Stripe, or x402-compatible settlement into the merchant account. Use a tiny event pickup product and a real refund path.

Avoid

AgentCart as money handler

If AgentCart accepts funds and remits later, it stops being a simple bridge and starts looking like a marketplace or payment intermediary.
